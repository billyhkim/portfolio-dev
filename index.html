Melleau

A mobile-first self-diagnostic mood tracking application to help users identify their emotions and bring control to their inner world

Melleau

I first came to the idea of building Melleau for our final group project to give people the opportunity to examine their mood and come to a deeper grasp of their inner world. Our team built the app so users could rate their mood, attach an emoji that best reflects their status, and provides room to journal their thoughts at the end.

Additionally, users are able to go back and view their entries with a chart that tracks their recent statuses, as well as check public testimonials from other users anonymously.

  • TECHNOLOGIESReact.js, JavaScript, Chart.js, PHP, phpMyAdmin, MySQL, Bootstrap, CSS3
  • AUDIENCEUsers seeking emotional awareness
  • LIVE WEBSITE STILL IN PROGRESS dev.melleau.net
  • GITHUB github.com/Learning-Fuze/c319_mood_tracker/tree/dev

Jed's Memory Match

A “PBS Kids” themed card-matching memory game with user-determined features and live updating statistics

Jed's Memory Match

This project stemmed from a personal desire to give my son, Jed, a fun game to play that would be appropriate for his age. The most rewarding part of the process was watching Jed enjoy the game and asking, "Dad, more Daniel Tiger please!"

The game provides live statistics based on the user's progress and the logic was converted from a procedural paradigm into OOP. The app is also desktop, tablet, and mobile responsive for users to enjoy on different devices.

  • TECHNOLOGIESJavaScript, HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, OOP format
  • AUDIENCEChildren
  • LIVE WEBSITEjedsmatch.billyhkim.dev
  • GITHUB github.com/billyhkim/jeds_match
